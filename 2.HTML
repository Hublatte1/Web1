CSS

Dependent sheets or Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) are languages ​​that describe how markup languages ​​are actually displayed. They are primarily used in HTML and XHTML, and can also be used in XML. It is a W3C standard and has a high degree of freedom in defining layouts and styles.

CSS3, which is currently under development, has added the ability to replace images created by graphic editing programs such as shadow effects, gradients, and transformations. It also adds a variety of animation features, replacing Adobe Flash to some extent.
